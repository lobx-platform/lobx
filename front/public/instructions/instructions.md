# Welcome to this study

<br>

The purpose of this study is to investigate the interaction between humans and machines (artificial intelligence agents) in electronic financial markets. You will be asked to participate in experimental financial markets where you can make decisions about buying or selling a virtual financial asset (not a real asset). During the study, we will collect data exclusively related to your buy or sell orders.

The study will last approximately 60 minutes. Participating is an excellent opportunity to understand how electronic financial markets work.

You will receive a show-up fee of 5 Euro. In addition, you can earn a considerable amount of extra money depending on your decisions.

<br>

## Instructions

<br>

Please follow the instructions carefully. If you have any questions, raise your hand and an experimenter will assist you.

You will participate in {numMarkets} markets where you can earn money by trading. Each market will last {marketDuration} minutes.

Your role is to trade the asset with the objective of earning as much money as possible. You may buy or sell shares during each market, and your earnings will depend on your trading decisions.

All other participants in your market are artificial traders (algorithms) that have no profit objective

One of these is an informed trader who with equal probability must carry out one of the following two objectives:

<br>

- Buy a certain number of shares at the lowest possible price

or

- Sell a certain number of shares at the highest possible price

<br>

You will not be informed of the objective assigned to this trader, i.e. whether they must sell or buy, nor the number of shares.

You can earn considerable money depending on your decisions. Your earnings will be transferred to your Prolific account.

<br>

## Initial Endowment and Action Limits

<br>

At the start of each market you will receive {initialCash} Liras and {initialStocks} shares.

Within this endowment you may buy and sell as you please (no short selling).

<br>


These limits also include **active passive orders**: cash and shares already committed to passive orders cannot be used for other orders, unless the passive order is first cancelled.

Example:

If you have **7 shares** and **7 active passive sell orders**, you cannot sell additional shares without first cancelling an order.

If you have **350 Liras** and **300 Liras** committed to passive buy orders, you can only use the remaining **50 Liras**.

<br>

## End-of-Market Rule

<br>

At the end of the market, you should hold the same number of shares as at the beginning.

If this is not the case, a penalty is applied. The penalty increases with the number of shares.

<br>

### Rules:

<br>

- If you have k extra shares, they will be sold at: **best selling price − k** 

- If you have k missing shares, you will have to buy them at: **best buying price + k** 

<br>

This means:

<br>

- Extra shares are sold at a lower price 

- Missing shares are bought at a higher price 

<br>

### Example (extra shares)

<br>

**You end the market with 2 extra shares.**

**The last best selling price is 110.**

Your shares are sold at: 110 − 2 = 108.
If you had sold earlier, you could have sold at 110. Your profits are reduced by 4.

<br>

### Example (missing shares):

<br>

**You end the market with 2 missing shares.**

The last best buying price is 110.  **You must buy them at: 110 + 2 = 112.**

If you had bought earlier, you could have bought at 110. Your profits are reduced by 4.

## The Trading Platform

<br>

The platform below is the interface you will use during the study.

The trading platform is a software that allows participants to trade with each other.

It displays relevant information on the supply and demand of an asset (e.g. a stock). 
The platform shows market information that is visible to all participants. It also shows your own orders and trades, which are visible only to you.

This trading platform closely replicates real financial market platforms, where participants trade in a double auction framework. 

This mechanism allows: 

<br>

- buyers to submit purchase offers (bids) 

- sellers to submit sale offers (asks) 

<br>

The platform matches buyers and sellers based on price and quantity.

On this platform the quantity of each order is limited to 1. If you want orders for larger quantities you must place multiple orders.

<br>

### Direct Orders (Aggressive Orders) 

<br>

You can directly accept an existing price to sell or buy, for example an order from another trader
In this case, the transaction is executed immediately at the best available bid or ask price.

<br>

### Passive Orders

<br>

You can enter buy or sell orders that are not executed immediately. In this case, you will need to wait for someone to accept them.
Note that some passive orders may improve the current best available price, but they are still passive orders.

Entering a sell order (ask) at a price lower than the current best ask. 

Entering a buy order (bid) at a price higher than the current best bid
